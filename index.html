New Instructional Initiatives

Publishing Guidelines

The landscape of online learning is continually, rapidly evolving. When creating online courses, today's instructors must consider a host of factors, including emerging pedagogical approaches and evolving educational technologies. Changes to academic regulations can also impact the design of digital courses and resources.

To help faculty in navigating this shifting terrain, the following guidelines are offered as a checklist of sorts to help them design and launch quality non-credit online learning projects at Notre Dame. These guidelines provide research-based rubrics and best practices drawn from institutions leading in the online learning space; they represent objective- and evidence-based standards for assessing projects; and they align strongly with the University's commitment to delivering high-quality non-credit online learning experiences, supported by appropriate resources and oversight.

Course Overview & Introduction

Providing a clear introduction as an instructor and an overview of the course structure and policies benefits students by establishing a positive learning environment, clarifying expectations, fostering trust and communication, and ensuring a smooth and organized learning experience.

  • Introduce yourself in person to the class.
  • Provide a digital version of your introduction on the course website.
  • Clearly state the purpose of the course
  • Clearly delineate the structure of the course
  • Clearly state expectations for online communication, including discussions, email and other forms of interaction
  • Clearly document course- and institutional policies with which the learner must comply
  • Clearly outline any required prerequisite knowledge, including technical and/or digital information literacies
  • Provides easy access to online support resources, inlcuding
    • Technical support
    • Accessibility policies and services
    • Care and wellness support
    • Academic support services and resources

Learning Objectives (Competencies)

Providing students with clear information about learning objectives and the competencies students will develop in the course offers a clear sense of direction, fostering motivation and enabling them to align their efforts and track their progress effectively.

  • Outline course objectives right away — on the course home page, if possible.
  • Ensure course objectives are clearly defined, measurable, and aligned to specific learning activities and assessments.
  • Specify learning objectives at the module-level.
  • Ensure learning objectives are measurable and that they contribute to the course-level objectives.
  • Ensure both course- and specific module-level learning objectives are suited to the course level.

Learning Activities & Instructional Materials

Providing students with clear information about learning activities and easy access to instructional materials enhances their understanding, engagement, and preparedness, and ultimately leads to improved learning outcomes. Always ensure course resources are subject-appropriate and engaging to students.

  • Help students under how instructional materials help them complete learning activities.
  • Model academic integrity by properly sourcing your instructional materials.
  • Test course resources and tools are accessible and reliable before instroducing them to students.
  • Ensure assignments engage higher-order thinking and problem-solving skills with critical reflection and analysis.
  • Support knowledge transfer by implementing real-world applications and activities, such as experiential learning and case studies.
  • Ensure instructional materials are based on the latest theory and practice in the discipline.
  • Facilitate active learning through interactive activities and team projects.

Assessment & Measurement

By developing and sharing a clear means of assessing and measuring students' progress and performance on assignments in the course, instructors provide students with a structured framework for understanding their strengths, areas of improvement, and overall growth, fostering a more effective learning environment and facilitating personalized feedback for enhancing their academic success.

  • Measure whether students are achieving the stated learning objectives (competencies).
  • Ensure assessments match the level of the stated objectives (competencies) for the course.
  • Provide learners with multiple activities and opportunitiesto track and assess their learning progress during the course.
  • Provide frequent, substantive, and timely feedback on learner performance

Interaction & Communcation

Providing clear policies and best practices for communicating and interacting with instructors and fellow students benefits everyone by fostering a positive and respectful learning environment, encouraging effective collaboration, and ensuring efficient communication channels, ultimately enhancing the overall educational experience.

  • State and model expectations for course interactions in all communication channels.
  • State and model required response times, providing prompt feedback on assignments.
  • Promote interactive learning behaviors in live sessions whenever applicable.
  • Ensure students come to know the instructor as both instructor and fellow learner.
  • Demonstrate the value of collaboration through learner-to-learner interactions.

Course Technology

Providing a clear introduction to course technology offers students the benefits of enhanced understanding, increased engagement, and improved proficiency in utilizing digital tools for communicating and learning.

  • State minimum technology requirements for the course.
  • Provide clear information on how to obtain and use required technologies.
  • Ensure required technologies support the learning objectives or competencies for the course.
  • Provide learners with information on protecting their data and privacy.
  • Ensure all technologies meet accessibility standards.
  • Provide vendor accessibility statements for required technologies.

Course Design & Layout
Inclusivity, Accessibility, & Usability

Providing students with a Course Design built to include principles of Inclusivity, Accessibility, and best practices of Usability, instructors ensure equal opportunities for all students, promote a diverse and inclusive learning environment, enhance accessibility for students with disabilities, and optimize the learning experience for maximum engagement and understanding.

  • Maintain consistent styles and navigation to that the online course easy to navigate and use.
  • Make all LMS pages and all course resources and materials accessible for diverse learners.
  • Use a standard style across your online course, carrying it through module-to-module.
  • Provide alternative means of accessing multimedia content for diverse learners.
  • Model careful work by ensuring course materials are free of grammatical, spelling, and other errors.
  • Create a logical, clear, and consistent course structure.
  • Carefully 'chunk' information into manageable sections.
  • Use a standard sans-serif font of at least 12 points.
